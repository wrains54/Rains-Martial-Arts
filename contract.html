Our Working Agreement

Plain language. No surprises. Here’s exactly what to expect when you partner with Cottagelady for your estate sale.

The information on this page provides a general overview of our working relationship. A formal written agreement is provided and reviewed with every client before any work begins. Donna is always happy to answer questions — please don’t hesitate to reach out before, during, or after reviewing these terms.

1

Commission Structure

Cottagelady operates on a commission-only basis. We earn a percentage of the total gross sales generated during your estate sale. There are no flat fees, no staging fees, no advertising fees, and no day-of service charges. Our specific commission rate is discussed and agreed upon during the consultation and outlined clearly in the written contract.

2

Minimum Estate Value

To ensure a sale is viable for both parties, we typically require a minimum of approximately $5,000 in sellable items. During the free consultation walk-through, Donna will assess the contents of the home and give you an honest, upfront estimate. If a sale doesn’t make sense for your situation, she’ll tell you that too — no obligation either way.

3

Property Access During Preparation

Once the agreement is signed and preparation begins, Cottagelady will need regular access to the property for staging, pricing, organizing, and setup. We’ll coordinate a schedule with you. The property should remain secure and accessible to our team throughout the preparation period, which typically spans 1–2 weeks prior to the sale.

4

Item Removal Before the Sale

Family members may remove personal belongings, heirlooms, or items they wish to keep before staging begins. Once pricing and organization are underway, items should remain in place. Any planned pre-sale removals must be communicated and agreed upon in advance. This protects the integrity of the sale and ensures accurate record-keeping.

5

Advertising & Promotion Rights

Cottagelady reserves the right to photograph, list, and advertise your estate sale through appropriate channels including EstateSales.net, social media, and local publications. Photography is conducted respectfully and focuses on items rather than personal or identifying details. Sale listings are designed to attract maximum buyer interest and are always reviewed carefully before publishing.

6

Payment to the Client

Following the close of the sale, Cottagelady will prepare a complete itemized accounting of all items sold and the total gross proceeds. After deducting the agreed commission, the remaining balance is paid to the client promptly — typically within 3–5 business days of sale completion. We believe in full transparency, and you’ll always know exactly what sold and for how much.

7

Unsold Items After the Sale

Items remaining after the sale concludes are the client’s responsibility unless otherwise agreed upon in advance. Cottagelady can assist with coordinating donations to local charities, connecting you with buyout services for bulk remainders, or advising on next steps. Your preferences will be discussed and documented before the sale begins so there are no loose ends when it’s over.

Questions About the Agreement?

Donna is always happy to walk through the details personally. A phone call or in-person visit is often the easiest way to get clear answers and a good feel for how everything works.
