Your Questions, Answered

Everything you need to know about working with Cottagelady — from first contact to final payment.

How do I know if an estate sale is right for my situation? +
An estate sale is a great fit when you need to clear a significant portion of a home’s contents — whether due to downsizing, moving into assisted living, settling an estate after a loss, or a major life change. If you’re unsure, simply call Donna. She’ll give you her honest assessment, even if an estate sale turns out not to be the right option for your situation. There’s never any pressure to proceed.
Is there a minimum value of items required to hold a sale? +
We typically require a minimum estate value of approximately $5,000 in sellable items to make the sale worthwhile for both parties. During your free consultation, Donna will walk through the home and give you an honest estimate of the contents’ potential value. There’s no charge for this visit and no obligation to move forward.
Are there any upfront fees or costs to me? +
Absolutely not. Cottagelady operates on a pure commission basis. We earn a percentage of total sales proceeds — nothing is charged upfront and there are no hidden fees. If the sale doesn’t perform, neither do we. Our interests are completely aligned with yours. The exact commission percentage is outlined in our agreement, which Donna will review with you before anything is signed.
How long does the whole process take? +
After the consultation and agreement are in place, staging and pricing typically take 1–2 weeks. The actual sale runs 2–3 days (usually Thursday–Saturday or Friday–Sunday). Payment to you follows within a few days of the sale’s close, along with a complete itemized accounting of what sold and at what price. Start to finish, the full process typically takes 3–5 weeks.
Do I need to be present during the sale? +
No — and many clients find it emotionally easier not to be. Donna and her team handle everything: staffing, checkout, crowd management, and security. You’re welcome to stop by if you’d like, but it’s completely optional. Many families choose to spend sale weekend elsewhere and simply receive their check when it’s done.
What percentage of items typically sell? +
Across Cottagelady’s 26+ completed sales, the average sell-through rate is 90–95% of all items. This is significantly higher than industry averages, thanks to Donna’s careful, research-based pricing and professional advertising that brings the right buyers to each sale. Items that are priced fairly and presented well consistently find new homes.
How do you price items? +
Donna uses a combination of current market research, knowledge of regional buyer trends, and years of hands-on experience with antiques, furniture, collectibles, jewelry, art, and everyday household items. The goal is always fair-market pricing — not bargain-basement, and not overpriced. We want items to sell, not just sit. For specialty or high-value pieces, additional research or outside appraisals may be recommended.
Can family members take items before the sale? +
Yes — with advance coordination. Family members are welcome to take personal items, heirlooms, or things set aside before staging begins. However, once pricing and staging are underway, items should remain in place to protect the integrity of the sale. We ask that all removal plans be communicated and agreed upon before our preparation starts.
What happens to items that don’t sell? +
Unsold items can be handled several ways depending on your preference. We can coordinate donation to local charities or organizations, arrange a buyout with a secondary estate buyer, or leave the remaining items for the family. We’ll discuss your preferences upfront and make sure the home is left in good order after the sale concludes.
What if we discover more items after the initial walk-through? +
It happens all the time! Drawers, closets, attics, and garages often reveal items that weren’t on the initial radar. If you find additional items after staging has begun, just let Donna know and she’ll determine the best way to incorporate them. Adding items during staging is usually fine; adding large quantities right before opening day may not be possible, but we always try to be flexible.
How do I get started? +
The easiest way is to call Donna directly at (931) 644-9963 or use the contact form on this website. She’ll schedule a free, no-pressure walk-through of your home, answer all your questions, and give you a clear picture of what an estate sale could look like for your situation. There’s never any obligation to proceed after the consultation.
What areas of Tennessee do you serve? +
Cottagelady primarily serves Middle Tennessee, including Murfreesboro, Nashville, Smyrna, Franklin, Brentwood, Columbia, La Vergne, and surrounding communities. If you’re outside this area, please reach out and Donna will let you know if she can accommodate your situation or refer you to a trusted colleague in your region.

Still have questions? Donna would love to hear from you.
